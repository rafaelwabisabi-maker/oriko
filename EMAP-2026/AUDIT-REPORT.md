## PART 2: DIMENSION-BY-DIMENSION AUDIT

### 🔬 SCIENTIFIC — Current score: 65/100

**What's working:**
- Shannon framing is original and sets up the question cleanly
- PEMF as scientific anchor is smart (FDA-approved, peer-reviewed)
- Mancuso/plant neurobiology connection legitimizes plants as sensors
- Crossover experimental design (4 × 10-day blocks) shows methodological rigor
- "Either result is publishable" = sophisticated scientific positioning

**What's broken:**

1. **THE EM MECHANISM IS UNEXPLAINED.** The proposal says AI models "generate electromagnetic frequency outputs" — but this is not how language models work. A jury scientist will stop reading. The actual chain is: **AI generates numerical sequences → converted to frequency parameters → drives Arduino → powers electromagnetic coil → produces EM field.** This one sentence is missing and its absence makes the core mechanism look like hand-waving.

2. **No species named.** "Species selected in consultation with a botanist" is vague. Arabidopsis thaliana (the standard lab model plant — fast-growing, well-mapped genetically, used in almost all plant bioelectricity research) should be named. Naming a species = proves scientific literacy.

3. **No PEMF citations named.** Should reference 1–2 specific papers (e.g., Radhakrishnan 2019 on EM fields and seed germination, Mancuso's bioelectric measurement protocols).

4. **Statistical threshold stated in technical plan but not proposal.** "p < 0.05 repeated-measures ANOVA" should appear (briefly) in the proposal text to signal rigorous experimental design.

**Fix priority: CRITICAL.** One paragraph will solve all of this.

### ❤️ EMOTIONAL — Current score: 45/100

**What's working:**
- "Does meaning have mass?" is an excellent hook
- "The art is not the result. The art is the willingness to ask." — strong closer
- BLESS.EXE as live collective participation has emotional resonance

**What's broken:**

1. **The Reich story is the emotional heart of the entire project and it appears in the LAST paragraph as "a second layer of meaning."** This is backwards. The image of a government burning six tons of research — an Austrian scientist dying in federal prison — is one of the most visceral facts in the whole proposal. It should appear in paragraph 2, not paragraph 7.

2. **The opening is cold.** Starting with Shannon 1948 is intellectually correct but emotionally distant. The reader needs a human story before they're handed an information theory problem.

3. **The plants are abstractions.** "Two groups of twelve identical plants" — there is no sensory language. No mention of what they look like, what it feels like to watch them grow over 60 days, what it means to see two growth curves diverge (or not). The BioLab's previous winners made you FEEL the biology.

4. **No moment of personal stakes.** Why does Rafael care about this? The artist statement hints at it, but the main proposal keeps Rafael at arm's length from the material.

**Fix priority: HIGH.** Move Reich to paragraph 2. Add 2–3 sentences of sensory/personal language.

### 🌍 SOCIETAL / POLITICAL — Current score: 35/100

**What's working:**
- Decolonization angle is present ("African-Brazilian spiritual traditions that carry their own suppressed epistemic history")
- Epistemic restitution is named
- The seven traditions include non-Western knowledge systems

**What's broken:**

1. **One sentence in the last paragraph is not enough.** For a 2026 jury, epistemic justice and decolonization of science are primary considerations. The proposal buries this.

2. **The political dimension is never made explicit.** Who decides what counts as science? What happens when a government burns research? What does it mean that an AI trained on Umbanda and Spiritist traditions is running a European laboratory experiment? These questions are latent in the project but never voiced.

3. **The "citizen science" angle of BLESS.EXE is underdeveloped.** Thirty-two cities. Anonymous users. Their intention enters the experiment without their knowledge — which raises genuine consent questions the proposal briefly mentions ("genuine ethical questions about consent") but doesn't develop. This is actually a rich societal dimension.

4. **No mention of what happens if the hypothesis is confirmed.** If prayer-trained AI produces biologically different effects — what does that mean for how we think about sacred traditions? That's a profound societal question.

**Fix priority: HIGH.** One dedicated paragraph on epistemic politics.

### 💻 TECHNICAL — Current score: 75/100

**What's working:**
- Budget is solid and realistic (€3K of €4K, leaves reserve)
- 8-week timeline is well-structured and feasible
- Arduino-based EM generation is a real, buildable approach
- Crossover design shows technical rigor
- BioLab requirements are specific and reasonable

**What's broken:**

1. **The EM chain is missing from the proposal** (already flagged under scientific)

2. **BLESS.EXE technical capacity is understated.** The platform is LIVE. It has 32 cities. It is handling real traffic. The proposal calls it "already operational as a working prototype" once, buried in paragraph 4. This is the strongest proof-of-capacity element and should be higher and louder.

3. **The accumulator is described as "sculptural antenna of layered organic and metallic materials designed after historical electromagnetic research from the 1940s."** This is deliberately vague (to avoid "orgone" triggering a juror). It's the right call — but the vagueness makes it sound like the artist doesn't know what they're building. One more technical detail (e.g., "alternating layers of organic fibres and metallic mesh, designed to create a gradient impedance boundary for low-frequency EM fields") would help.

**Fix priority: MEDIUM.** Small additions.

3. **No mention of the Ars Electronica Festival 2026 context** — the installation could become an Ars Electronica exhibit, which is a strong additional pitch for the host.

**Fix priority: MEDIUM.** Two sentences.
