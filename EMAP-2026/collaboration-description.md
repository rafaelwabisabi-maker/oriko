# Collaboration Description
## PRAYER RESIDUE — EMAP 2026

---

## Collaboration Structure

PRAYER RESIDUE operates as a three-layer collaboration:

**Layer 1: Artist Duo — Rafael Vaz + Elisabeth Braunschmid**

Rafael leads concept development, AI system design, accumulator fabrication, and the BLESS.EXE digital platform. Elisabeth co-designs the installation aesthetics, leads documentation and data visualization design, and coordinates the exhibition presentation. Both are based in Linz, Austria.

The collaboration between Rafael and Elisabeth is grounded in complementary practices: Rafael's work moves between AI systems and Afro-Brazilian cultural memory; Elisabeth's practice centers on material design, spatial installation, and community-facing cultural production. Together they have co-developed multiple projects in the Linz cultural ecosystem.

**Layer 2: Proposed Scientific Collaborator — Plant Biologist / Biophysicist**

The measurement protocol at the heart of the experiment requires scientific expertise in plant physiology. We are seeking a plant biologist or biophysicist — ideally from Johannes Kepler University Linz (JKU) or BOKU Vienna — to co-design the experimental methodology, validate the measurement approach, and co-author any resulting publication.

The collaborator's role is specific: they design how growth rate, bioelectric signals, and chlorophyll content are measured. They determine sample size, control conditions, and statistical analysis approach. They review the data. They ensure the experiment meets peer-review standards.

The artist's role is equally specific: concept design, AI system, accumulator fabrication, digital platform, and installation. The boundary between artistic and scientific responsibility is clear and productive.

We request Ars Electronica's support in identifying a suitable scientific collaborator from their existing network or from the regional academic institutions in Linz and Upper Austria.

**Layer 3: Distributed Collaboration — BLESS.EXE Community**

The BLESS.EXE platform creates a third layer of collaboration that is diffuse, anonymous, and global. Users who submit blessings during the residency period become, without knowing it, participants in the experiment — their collective intentional data feeds the prayer-trained AI model in real time. This raises genuine ethical questions about consent, aggregated intention, and the boundaries of participation — questions the project addresses directly as part of its conceptual framework.

---

## Why This Collaboration Structure Works for Ars Electronica

The BioLab at Ars Electronica has hosted three consecutive EMAP residencies (2022–2024) that share a pattern: artist + scientist + living biology + genuine experimental question. PRAYER RESIDUE fits this pattern precisely — with the added dimension that the artist already lives in Linz, has direct access to the local academic network, and brings a cultural perspective (Brazilian spiritual traditions) that no Austrian or European artist can replicate.
