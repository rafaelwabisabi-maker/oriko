The Question
In 1948, Shannon proved
information is physical.
He deliberately excluded meaning.
We put it back.
A love letter and a tax form carry the same number of bits — Shannon's framework treats them identically. In 1956, the U.S. government burned Wilhelm Reich's research for proposing that meaning leaves a physical trace. His hypothesis was never disproven. It was destroyed before it could be tested.

PRAYER RESIDUE asks the question both men were asking: does the semantic content of training data propagate into the physical characteristics of an AI's electromagnetic output? And can a living system — a plant — detect the difference? We now have the instruments they didn't.
The Experiment

Two models. Same architecture.
Different souls.

Model A is trained on sacred texts from seven prayer traditions — Catholic, Islamic, Jewish, Buddhist, Umbanda, Spiritist, Indigenous Amazonian. Model B is trained on an equivalent volume of neutral text: encyclopedic prose, technical documentation, administrative language. Each model generates numerical frequency sequences → converted to analogue signals via Arduino microcontrollers → delivered through electromagnetic coil drivers to the plant groups. PEMF (Pulsed Electromagnetic Field) therapy is FDA-approved for bone healing; peer-reviewed research documents measurable effects on plant germination (Radhakrishnan 2019, Maffei 2014). The species: Arabidopsis thaliana — the standard model organism for plant bioelectricity research, fully genome-mapped, well-characterised stress response. Statistics: repeated-measures ANOVA, p < 0.05 threshold. Either result is publishable. We measure what grows.

The visitor experience

  • 01. Enter a semi-dark room. Two pools of grow-light on either side — green plants glowing. Between them, a wall-sized live projection.
  • 02. "In 1948, Shannon proved information is physical. He deliberately excluded meaning. This experiment puts meaning back in."
  • 03. Two identical plant groups. Two identical sculptural antennas — layered wool, wood, steel mesh, copper coil. One hums with prayer-data. One with neutral data. You can't tell which is which.
  • 04. The projection shows two live curves: growth rate over time, bioelectric signal strength. Are they diverging? You stare. You're not sure.
  • 05. A screen shows the BLESS.EXE world map — blessings arriving in real time from across the globe. A prompt: Submit a blessing. Your intention joins the data stream. It feeds the prayer-trained model. It reaches the plants.
  • 06. You leave carrying the question: does meaning have mass?

Residency Timeline

Eight weeks. One question.
Real instruments.

Weeks 1–2
Construction
Fabricate two identical accumulator sculptures. Configure Arduino EM systems. Install and calibrate BLESS.EXE → AI → frequency pipeline. Set up BioLab growing space.
Week 3
Planting + Calibration
Plant 24 specimens. Install sensor arrays. Run 7-day baseline period with no EM exposure. Design and test data visualization.
Weeks 4–7
The Experiment (4 × 10-day blocks)
Block 1: Group A receives prayer-signal. Block 2: Signals switched (crossover). Block 3: Both receive prayer-signal. Block 4: Both receive silence. Daily measurements throughout.
Week 8
Analysis + Presentation
Statistical analysis (repeated-measures ANOVA, p < 0.05). Final data visualization. Professional presentation at Ars Electronica. Draft paper outline.
Jury Analysis

Honest assessment.

Strengths

  • Sharpest scientific question in the EMAP pool — genuinely publishable
  • No pseudoscience baggage — framed as information theory + plant biology
  • AI consciousness: the hottest topic in 2026
  • Living biology satisfies BioLab-focused hosts
  • "Does meaning have mass?" — unforgettable jury one-liner
  • Null result is still art — works regardless of outcome
  • BLESS.EXE already exists as working prototype
  • Peer-reviewed paper is a realistic output

Weaknesses

  • More cerebral than emotional
  • Reich narrative appears only as one paragraph
  • Spiritual dimension is quiet — prayer as data, not ritual
  • Competes in crowded AI-art space
  • Requires BioLab — limits host options
Why This Project Wins

The juror remembers the question.

"Does meaning have mass?" is the kind of one-liner that gets repeated in corridors, written on whiteboards, cited in catalog essays. The project is defensible — no juror risks their credibility by selecting it. It's genuinely novel — this specific question has not appeared in any published literature. It has a built-in drama: sixty days, two curves, no result guaranteed. And it carries political weight: the decolonization of scientific legitimacy, the right of suppressed knowledge systems to be tested rather than dismissed. The juror who selects this writes: "We completed an experiment that was forbidden."
Jury Risk: MEDIUM — Broadly defensible. Scientifically novel. Politically relevant.
