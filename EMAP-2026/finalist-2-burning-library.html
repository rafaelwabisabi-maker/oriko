THE BURNING
LIBRARY

Seventy Years Late.
"All orgone energy accumulators and all components thereof shall be destroyed by crushing, cutting, or other suitable means — all written materials shall be burned in a public incinerator."
U.S. District Court Injunction — 1954 / Books burned by FDA — 1956
The Story

They burned the books.
They jailed the scientist.
They never ran the experiment.

Wilhelm Reich was an Austrian scientist — educated in Vienna, once a colleague of Freud. He proposed that layered organic and metallic materials concentrate a biological energy he called orgone. His laboratory was in Maine. His hypothesis was never tested with modern instruments. In 1956, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration supervised the burning of six tons of his research materials and books. Reich died in federal prison in 1957, one year before completing his sentence.

A Brazilian artist living in Linz rebuilds his apparatus. Feeds it with Afro-Brazilian spiritual traditions via AI. Runs the experiment. Seventy years late.
Timeline of Suppression

What was destroyed.

1897
Wilhelm Reich born in Austria Educated in Vienna. Student of Freud. Physician, researcher, psychiatrist. Begins investigating the intersection of biological energy and psychological wellbeing.
1940s
The Accumulator Reich designs and patents the orgone accumulator — a layered structure of organic and metallic materials. Publishes experimental protocols. Claims measurable biological effects in plant growth and immune response.
1954
Injunction Issued The U.S. FDA obtains a federal court injunction ordering the destruction of all orgone accumulators and related materials. Reich refuses to comply. He continues the research.
1956
Books Burned FDA officials supervise the burning of approximately six tons of Wilhelm Reich's publications and research materials in a public incinerator in New York. First book-burning by a U.S. federal agency in the 20th century.
1957
Death in Prison Reich dies of heart failure in Lewisburg Federal Penitentiary, November 3, 1957. One year before the end of his sentence. The experiment was never formally completed.
2026
The Experiment Runs A Brazilian artist in Linz rebuilds the apparatus to Reich's 1940s specifications. Feeds it with AI-generated electromagnetic signals trained on Afro-Brazilian prayer traditions — the spiritual legacy of another colonized people. Plants grow beneath it. Sensors measure the result. The art is the completion.
The Core Visual

Two objects.
One story.

The installation's heart is a confrontation: the destroyed apparatus and the rebuilt one, side by side. Charred ruin. Humming life.
Object 1 — Destroyed
The Charred Ruin
A replica of the original accumulator, built to Reich's specifications — then deliberately charred and damaged. Placed on a pedestal. Cold. Silent. A label reads: "Reich's laboratory equipment was destroyed under federal supervision. His books were burned in a public incinerator. He died in prison in 1957."
→
Object 2 — Rebuilt
The Living Structure
An identical accumulator — but alive. Warm light. A hum. Layered wool, wood, steel mesh, copper coil. Below it: twelve plants, growing, wired with sensors. The BLESS.EXE stream feeds it in real time — Umbanda, Islam, Buddhism, Judaism arriving as data. The label: "Rebuilt by a Brazilian artist in Linz, 2026. Feeding Afro-Brazilian prayer traditions through AI. Running the experiment seventy years late."

The visitor experience

  • 01. Enter near-darkness. The first thing you see: a wall of white Courier text on black — the FDA court order, projected floor-to-ceiling. "...shall be destroyed."
  • 02. Turn left. A charred object on a pedestal — a replica of the original accumulator, blackened, crumbling. A label with the facts of the burning and the death.
  • 03. Turn right. An identical structure — but this one glows. Warm. Humming. Below it: twelve plants, green, wired with sensors. Alive.
  • 04. An archive table: timeline, biography, list of burned texts, FDA correspondence — printed on paper, available to hold.
  • 05. BLESS.EXE terminal glows beside it. Blessings arriving from Umbanda, Islam, Buddhism, Judaism. You submit one. It feeds the rebuilt machine. It feeds the plants.
  • 06. Exit. The question is not "does it work?" The question is: why was it forbidden?

Jury Analysis

Honest assessment.

Strengths
  • Most emotionally powerful concept in the pool
  • Strongest narrative — book-burning + imprisonment + restitution
  • Political dimension — state suppression of science + epistemic injustice
  • Decolonization is structural: Brazilian traditions reclaiming Austrian science
  • Destroyed/rebuilt pair is an unforgettable visual
  • Media potential — magazine feature, documentary, controversy
  • Ars Electronica: Reich was Austrian. Linz is home. Locality IS the art.
Weaknesses
  • "Orgone" is unavoidable — some jurors will flinch
  • Pseudoscience association — jurors risk credibility
  • Polarizing — there is no "maybe" pile
  • Harder to find a scientist willing to associate
  • Career risk for Rafael: "the orgone artist" is a label
Why This Project Wins

Nobody else will submit anything like this.

In a pool of 500 applications, most are digital art, most are safe, most are forgettable. THE BURNING LIBRARY is unforgettable. The juror who selects it gets to write: "We are completing an experiment that was forbidden." That's a career-defining curatorial statement. The juror either selects it in the first round or rejects it — there is no "maybe" pile.
Jury Risk: HIGH — Maximum distinction. Maximum polarization. If your juror loves it, you win.
