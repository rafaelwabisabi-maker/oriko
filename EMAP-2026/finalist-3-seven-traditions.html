The Concept

A living experiment in
sacred geography.

Three billion people pray daily. Different words. Different gestures. Different gods. But the same question: does intention reach beyond the body?

SEVEN TRADITIONS gives that question real instruments. Seven AI models, each trained on a different prayer tradition, each generating a unique electromagnetic frequency. Seven plant groups grow in a mandala arrangement — one group beneath each tradition's signal. An eighth group grows in silence. Sixty days. The data is displayed as a living overhead mandala of growth curves, color-coded by tradition, visible in real time. The question is not which tradition is "right." The question is whether any of them — all of them — leave a different mark.

The Installation

Walk through a
living mandala.

The visitor experience

  • 01. Enter a dark room. Seven glowing circles of colored light arranged in a mandala. Quiet overlapping hum of frequencies. Immediately: a sacred space.
  • 02. Each circle is a raised plinth: six small plants, a sculptural EM antenna, a colored LED ring, and a label with a prayer fragment in its original script.
  • 03. Walk the mandala. Rose gold Catholic. Emerald Islamic. Amber Jewish. Saffron Buddhist. Violet Umbanda. Silver Spiritist. Ochre Indigenous.
  • 04. The grey center: the control group. No antenna. No signal. A label: "What grows without prayer?"
  • 05. Look up. The ceiling projection: eight growth curves in eight colors, radiating from center. Some lines climbing. Some flat. Are the prayer-traditions outgrowing the control? Is any tradition more effective than another? The question becomes uncomfortable.
  • 06. BLESS.EXE terminal. Choose a tradition. Submit a blessing. Watch it appear on the world map. Your intention feeds one of the seven antennas.
  • 07. Exit. Part temple. Part greenhouse. Part data center. The question: do all prayers grow the same?

Jury Analysis

Honest assessment.

Strengths
  • Most visually stunning concept — the mandala overhead shot is unforgettable
  • Most "universal-spiritual" — all seven traditions treated equally, with reverence
  • Strongest visitor experience — 5-minute immersive walk-through
  • Decolonization is structural: Umbanda and Amazonian sit equal to Catholicism
  • Multi-script typography unique: Arabic, Hebrew, Sanskrit, Yoruba, Tupi
  • The uncomfortable question: "do all prayers grow the same?" is generative
  • Sound design: 7 overlapping tones, felt more than heard
  • BLESS.EXE integration is seamless and participatory
Weaknesses
  • Most expensive: 8 plant groups, 7 antennas, LED system, overhead projection
  • Most complex to build in 8 weeks — 7 EM stations is ambitious
  • Statistical power diluted: 6 plants per group (vs. 12) weakens data
  • "Ranking prayers" — could be read as comparing religions
  • Less publishable: 7 conditions with n=6 is underpowered for peer review
  • Requires large space — 10m × 10m minimum
Why This Project Wins

When the juror looks at the key visual — they stop scrolling.

An overhead shot of seven glowing circles of plants in seven sacred colors arranged in a mandala in a dark room. No other application in the pool looks like this. The experience of walking through it is singular: part temple, part greenhouse, part data center. And the question it poses — do all traditions leave the same mark? — is one that every human being finds personally relevant. It doesn't need a manifesto. It speaks.
Jury Risk: LOW-MEDIUM — Broadly appealing. Visually unforgettable.
