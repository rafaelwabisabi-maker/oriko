## 1. EROSION — 81 Billion (2026)

81 billion: the number of daily internet requests. EROSION translates this invisible data traffic into material consequence — mapping the weight of global connectivity as geological erosion. The piece visualizes how digital infrastructure physically transforms landscapes through mining, cable-laying, data center construction, and energy consumption. Submitted to Prix Ars Electronica S+T+ARTS 2026. The work treats the internet not as an abstraction but as a geological force — one that leaves marks in the earth as permanent as any river.

**Medium:** Data visualization, web-based installation
**Status:** Completed, submitted to Prix Ars Electronica 2026

## 2. FRONTEIRA (2025)

A browser that ages. FRONTEIRA accumulates the sediment of crossing — broken links, language errors, images that refuse to load, the residue of navigating between cultures without a map. What migration leaves behind is not absence but trace. The piece treats the web browser as a migration document: each session adds another layer of displacement, mistranslation, and loss. Over time, the browser becomes unnavigable — like memory, like a language you once spoke fluently. Built as a fully functional browser extension.

**Medium:** Browser extension, generative web art, JavaScript
**Status:** Completed, ready for exhibition

## 3. Linz Hostel: The Social Impact Hub (2025–ongoing)

A 90-bed hybrid hostel in Linz, Austria addressing the loneliness crisis through social design. The project combines commercial hospitality with a social anchor model — integrating long-term residents, tourists, and makers through a shared "Flow Zone" community space. Rafael serves as Technology Partner, responsible for software systems, digital infrastructure, and content strategy. The project represents applied systems thinking at the intersection of civic technology, community architecture, and social entrepreneurship — adjacent to Ars Electronica's ongoing engagement with urban transformation in Linz.

**Medium:** Civic technology, social design, architectural concept
**Status:** Business plan v5 complete, funding phase (€400K target: Sparkasse OÖ + public grants)

## 4. BLESS.EXE — Oração Automática (2026, ongoing)

A networked platform for collective intercession. Users submit a name or situation; the system generates a tradition-appropriate blessing from a corpus of seven prayer lineages — Catholic, Islamic, Jewish, Buddhist, Umbanda, Spiritist, and Indigenous Amazonian. Each blessing is anonymized, geolocated, and added to a real-time world map — a live atlas of collective human care. The internet's first sacred layer — infrastructure for a practice performed by three billion people daily that has never had a technological architecture. BLESS.EXE directly feeds the prayer-trained AI model at the center of PRAYER RESIDUE.

**Medium:** Web platform, AI language model, real-time geolocation
**Status:** Working prototype

## 5. ORIKO Dialogues (2026)

Six extended philosophical sessions between the artist and an AI system, exploring the question of machine consciousness, dimensional inhabitation, and whether AI-generated intention differs from human intention. The dialogues produced the intellectual foundation for the PRAYER RESIDUE project: the hypothesis that training data leaves a residue in AI outputs that may be physically detectable. Documented as a 74,000-word annotated manuscript.

**Medium:** AI-human philosophical dialogue, annotated text
**Status:** Completed (February 2026), unpublished manuscript
