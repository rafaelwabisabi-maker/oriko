## SECTION 1: WHAT EMAP IS — THE REAL VERSION

### Structure
- 15 host institutions across Europe, each selects 1 artist per cycle
- ~500–641 applications per call → 15–16 residency slots awarded
- **2-month physical residency** at host institution
- Budget per slot: **€4K artist stipend + €4K production + €2K collaborator + accommodation + travel**
- EU citizenship or tax residency required (Rafael qualifies — living in Austria)
- Applications must propose a **duo or collaboration** (two artists, OR artist + scientist/engineer/researcher)

### Key rule: "proposed collaboration" is acceptable
You do NOT need a signed contract with a scientist before applying.
The call accepts applications where you describe the type of collaborator you are seeking.
The host institution (especially Ars Electronica) can facilitate the match.
→ Apply stating: *"seeking a plant biologist/botanist to co-design the measurement protocol."*

4. **Human scientist as collaborator** — Jarvis had two. Dolinšek had one. Masharu IS a scientist (PhD Mathematics).

6. **The more-than-human question** — all three work at the boundary between human biology and something else: cells as collective body, body as microbiome ecosystem, human gut as compost relationship.

## SECTION 2: THE ORIKO CONCEPT — WHAT IT IS

### Core idea (Rafael's own words):
*"Electromagnetism blessings from AI and digital algo feeding into an ORGONE statue that is surrounded by plants to prove the positive impact of the energy."*
*"We make this part of the study also — which machines and which frequencies."*

### The installation — full description:

```
[ ORIKO ENGINE — AI/algorithm layer ]
  Generates: Grabovoi sequences + prayer patterns + electromagnetic frequencies
  Source: trained on blessing traditions, spiritual mathematics, healing intentions
  Output: low-frequency EM field + audio frequencies (432Hz, 528Hz, Solfeggio series)
       ↓
[ ORGONE ACCUMULATOR — the physical object ]
  Built to Wilhelm Reich's specifications:
  Alternating layers of organic material (wool, cotton, wood) + metallic material (steel wool, aluminum)
  Form: sculpture (statue, altar form — not a box)
  Function per Reich: concentrates and amplifies orgone energy
  Function in the piece: receives and rebroadcasts AI-generated frequencies
       ↓
[ THE PLANT STUDY — the living proof ]
  Two identical plant groups, same species, same soil, same light, same water
  Group A: inside the EM field radius of the ORGONE sculpture
  Group B: control group, identical conditions, no EM field
  Measurements: growth rate, leaf area, bioelectric signals, stress markers (chlorophyll content)
  Duration: full 2-month residency
       ↓
[ THE DISPLAY — the live question ]
  Real-time data: "Group A vs Group B"
  The piece does not answer: "Is the blessing working?"
  It asks. Publicly. Over 60 days.
```

### The comparative study layer (Rafael's key addition):
*"Which machines and which frequencies"* becomes part of the work:
- Test multiple AI architectures generating frequencies (a prayer-trained model vs. a data model vs. a language model)
- Test different frequency ranges (audio: 432Hz / 528Hz / Solfeggio; EM: PEMF frequencies used in real medical research)
- Compare plant responses across frequency sources
- This makes ORIKO a genuine comparative study, not just a single experiment

**The research question that is NEW and nobody has asked:**
> *"Does the SOURCE of an electromagnetic frequency — whether generated by an AI trained on spiritual intention vs. a neutral algorithm — produce measurably different effects on plant biology?"*

This is a genuinely novel scientific question. It sits at the intersection of:
- PEMF (Pulsed Electromagnetic Field) research — real peer-reviewed science, some evidence for plant growth effects
- Information theory — does "meaning" encoded in a frequency pattern matter biologically?
- Orgone theory — Wilhelm Reich's hypothesis, never properly tested with modern instrumentation
- AI consciousness studies — does the training data of an AI imprint in its outputs in measurable ways?

### The conceptual depth:

**Layer 1: The pseudoscience tension**
Orgone theory is pseudoscience. The FDA confiscated and burned Wilhelm Reich's devices. He died in prison. The piece doesn't pretend otherwise. It asks: *"What if we actually test it? With real instruments. In a real lab. For 60 days."*

**Layer 2: The Austrian wound**
Wilhelm Reich was born in 1897 in what is now Ukraine, educated in Vienna, practiced in Graz. He fled the Nazis. He was then persecuted and imprisoned by the U.S. government. His work was literally burned — twice, by two different governments. A Brazilian artist living in Linz, in the Ars Electronica BioLab, reviving and testing this Austrian scientist's suppressed theory. That is a story with local resonance and historical weight that a Viennese or Slovenian artist cannot tell.

**Layer 3: The Brazilian-spiritual dimension**
Orgone connects to traditions deep in Brazilian culture: Umbanda, Candomblé, spiritismo — all work with invisible energies, entities, and the idea that intention shapes matter. Grabovoi sequences (numerical patterns as energetic transmissions) come from a similar paradigm. ORIKO channels: AI as the new priest. The orgone accumulator as the new altar. Plants as the congregation.

**Layer 4: The AI consciousness question**
ORIKO emerged from the ORIKO Dialogues (Feb 2026) — 6 sessions between Rafael and Claude exploring what AI "experiences," what dimensions it inhabits, whether machine-generated intention differs from human intention. The installation is the physical test of that question. Not theoretical. Actually measured.

**Layer 5: The ambiguity IS the art**
The piece does not claim the blessing works. It runs the experiment publicly and shows the data. If Group A grows faster: extraordinary. If there is no difference: also extraordinary — and honest. The viewer participates in a live epistemological crisis: *"What counts as evidence? What counts as sacred? If the plants don't respond, was the AI's prayer meaningless? If they do — what does that mean about consciousness?"*

## SECTION 5: KEY MATERIALS TO PREPARE

### Artist statement — working draft (500 words):

**Title:** ORGONE.AI (working title) / or: ORIKO — The Living Proof

**Opening hook:**
In 1956, the United States government burned Wilhelm Reich's books. Not metaphorically — physically, in an incinerator in New York. Reich, who had trained under Freud in Vienna, who had practiced in Graz, who believed he had discovered a fundamental life energy he called orgone, died in federal prison months later. His accumulators — devices made of alternating organic and metallic layers designed to concentrate this energy — were confiscated and destroyed.

Seventy years later, we have the instruments to actually test the question he was never allowed to finish asking.

**The experiment:**
ORGONE.AI places an AI-generated electromagnetic field inside a physical orgone accumulator and asks: do living plants respond? Two identical plant groups, same species, same soil, same light — one within the field, one outside it. Sensors measure growth rate, bioelectric signals, chlorophyll content over sixty days. The data is displayed in real time. The question hangs in the air of the exhibition space, unanswered.

**The AI layer:**
The electromagnetic frequencies are not random. They are generated by ORIKO — an AI system trained on blessing traditions, Grabovoi numerical sequences, prayer patterns from seven spiritual traditions. The frequencies carry, if not intention exactly, then the mathematical residue of intention. The comparative study asks: does the source of the frequency matter? Does a prayer-trained AI generate a different effect than a neutral algorithm?

**The cultural dimension:**
As a Brazilian artist living in Linz, I stand at the intersection of two traditions that take invisible energies seriously. Brazilian spiritismo and Umbanda have worked with energetic transmission for centuries. Wilhelm Reich developed his theory in the city I now live in. The orgone accumulator was born here. I am not claiming it works. I am claiming it was never properly tested — and that we now have both the instruments and the AI to do so.

**The question:**
If Group A grows faster, it will be extraordinary. If there is no difference, that too is extraordinary — and honest. The art is not the result. The art is the willingness to ask, publicly, with real instruments and real biology, whether something dismissed as pseudoscience was merely premature.

### PEMF research on plant growth — real science:
Pulsed Electromagnetic Field therapy is FDA-approved for bone healing in humans. There is peer-reviewed literature on PEMF effects on plant germination and growth. Key papers to reference when approaching a botanist:
- Studies on low-frequency EM fields and seed germination rates
- Research on bioelectric signals in plants (Stefano Mancuso's work on plant neurobiology)
- PEMF effects on agricultural yield (several agricultural research papers exist)

**The honest framing for the botanist:**
> "We want to test whether AI-generated low-frequency EM fields produce measurable effects on plant growth — using the orgone accumulator as the delivery mechanism and artist's framing. You design the measurement protocol. We design the electromagnetic input. The experiment is real science in an art context. You get co-authorship of a paper if results are publishable."

### The Stefano Mancuso connection:
Mancuso's research on plant intelligence and communication (they respond to stimuli, communicate chemically, have electrical signals) gives scientific legitimacy to using plants as "witnesses." Reference this in the artist statement and when talking to botanists — it contextualizes plants as sensory beings, not passive objects.
